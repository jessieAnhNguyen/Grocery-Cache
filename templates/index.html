If you ever want to taste what best Nepal has to offer, you need not look further than one of the most famous cuisine found in every corner of the country.

Mo:mo (рдо:рдо:) has made itself the unofficial official food of Nepal with different styles of preparing it and exotic flavours from each place you try it.

Here, we will try and give you a general way of preparing the famous steamed dumplings from Nepal but what's special about this dish is that you can change it to your liking to make it fit perfectly to what you desire.

Steps

  1. Mix together the flour and 1 1/2 cups room temperature water in a bowl. Knead the dough well until it is medium-firm and flexible. Cover and let rest for 1 hour.
  2. Heat butter and heat minced garlic and minced peeled ginger in the melter butter
  3. Mix the butter, garlic and ginger with the meat
  4. Mix onions, cilantro, salt, red chilly powder, cumin powder, ground cumin and oil with the meat in a bowl
  5. To make the wrappers: Break off 1/2 ounce of dough and forming it into a ball. Place the ball on a flat surface and roll it into a 4-inch round with a rolling pin. Repeat with the remaining dough.
  6. Spray a steamer pan with cooking spray.
  7. Place a tablespoon of the chicken filling in the middle of a wrapper. Holding the wrapper in your left hand. Use your right thumb and index finger to start pinching the edges of the wrapper together. Pinch and fold until the edges of the circle close up like a little satchel. Place the momo in the prepared steamer pan. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling.
  8. Fill the steamer pot halfway with water and bring to a boil. Set the steamer pan with the momos on top of the pot and cover with a tight lid. Steam the momos until cooked, 8 to 9 minutes.
